My research interests consist of two interrelated tracks within Human-Centered Computing: Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Human-Robot Interaction (HRI). These two parallel paths share much in terms of underlying themes and has led my research focus to addressing factors that negatively impact the ability of people to effectively use technology to augment their capabilities, for example improving the interaction of touch enabled devices to present critical task information on small displays. Specifically, I am interested in how ineffective interaction design and overall poor system design can compromise the performance of users.

Fine Motor Skills

Research Area:

Challenge/Problem: In future spacecraft, crew will be interacting with touch-based interfaces to a greater extent than ever before. As we develop these interfaces for specific use in long-duration missions, it is important to first understand how prolonged exposure to a microgravity environment may influence fine motor skills, which are crucial to successful operation of these interfaces.

Solution: To measure fine motor skills performance, a Fine Motor Skills test battery was developed, consisting of the following tasks: (a) multidirectional pointing task, in which participants tap targets of different sizes arranged in a circle; (b) dragging task, in which a target is dragged from one position to another; (c) shape-tracing task, in which a square and a circle are traced; and (d) pinch-rotate task, in which a square is resized and rotated using the thumb and index finger to align with an object presented on the screen.

Status: Currently testing astronauts fine motor skills using the FMS test battery on the International Space Station
